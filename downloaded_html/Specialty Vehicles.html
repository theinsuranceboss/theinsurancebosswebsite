Common Specialty Vehicle Insurance Questions

Everything you need to know about insuring your collector and exotic cars

What qualifies as a specialty vehicle? +

Vehicles typically 25+ years old, limited production models, exotic supercars, custom builds, or vehicles maintained primarily as collectibles qualify. The car must be in good or excellent condition, properly stored, and not used as a daily driver.

Examples include classic muscle cars, vintage European sports cars, pre-war automobiles, exotic supercars like Ferraris and Lamborghinis, custom hot rods, and rare limited-production vehicles.

How much does specialty vehicle insurance cost? +

Specialty vehicle insurance is typically 40-60% cheaper than standard auto insurance. For a $50,000 classic car, expect $300-800 annually versus $1,500-2,500 for standard coverage. Exact costs depend on agreed value, driving record, storage, and usage.

Multi-car discounts significantly reduce costs. Many collectors insure 3-5 vehicles for less than one daily driver.

What is agreed value coverage? +

Agreed value means you and the insurer agree upfront on your vehicle's value. If the car is totaled, you receive the full agreed amount without depreciation deductions. This contrasts with actual cash value policies that depreciate annually.

The agreed value is established through appraisals, recent sales data, and market comparisons. Most policies allow annual value updates to reflect appreciation.

Are there mileage restrictions? +

Most specialty policies have annual mileage limits between 2,500-7,500 miles, though some offer unlimited mileage. These limits are reasonable for collector cars driven to shows, events, and occasional pleasure drives.

If you exceed agreed mileage, you won't lose coverage, but you may pay additional premiums. Many insurers offer mileage upgrades.

Can I daily drive my specialty vehicle? +

Most specialty policies prohibit using the insured vehicle as primary transportation. You typically need a separate daily driver. However, occasional pleasure driving, errands, and limited commuting are usually permitted within mileage limits.

Some insurers offer "occasional use" policies allowing limited regular driving. Discuss this upfront to ensure proper coverage without violating policy terms.

Do I need special storage for my vehicle? +

Most policies require secure storage in a locked garage or storage facility when not in use. Some high-value vehicles may require climate-controlled storage or alarm systems. Street parking is typically prohibited.

Storage requirements vary by insurer and vehicle value. A $30,000 classic may only need a home garage, while a $500,000 exotic might require enclosed climate-controlled storage with security systems.

Are modifications and custom parts covered? +

Yes, modifications and custom parts are covered when disclosed and included in the agreed value. Engine swaps, custom paint, performance upgrades, and aftermarket parts are all coverable with proper documentation.

Document all modifications with receipts, photos, and appraisals. The agreed value should reflect the cost of customization.

Does specialty insurance cover track days or racing? +

Standard specialty policies typically exclude racing and competition use. However, many insurers offer optional track day coverage or specialized racing policies for HPDE events, time trials, and amateur racing.

Track coverage is available as an add-on or separate policy. Costs vary based on experience, vehicle value, and track type. Always verify coverage before track use.
