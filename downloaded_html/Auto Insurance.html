Complete Coverage Options

Your vehicle deserves reliable protection. Our auto insurance policies are built to safeguard you on the road and give you peace of mind every time you're behind the wheel.

Liability Coverage

Required in most states, this covers bodily injury and property damage you cause to others in an accident. It also protects your assets and includes legal defense if you're sued.

Collision Coverage

Pays for damage to your vehicle after a collision with another car or object regardless of fault. Essential for protecting the value of your vehicle.

Complete Coverage

Covers non-collision incidents such as theft, vandalism, fire, storms, falling objects, and animal impacts. Protects your car from life’s unexpected events.

Uninsured Motorist

Protects you if you're hit by a driver with little or no insurance or someone who leaves the scene. Covers medical bills and vehicle damage when the other party can't pay.

Medical Payments

Helps pay medical expenses for you and your passengers after an accident, no matter who is at fault. Includes hospital care, surgery, X-rays, and rehabilitation.

Roadside Assistance

Get support anytime with towing, jump-starts, lockout service, flat tire changes, and emergency fuel delivery. 24/7 peace of mind on the road.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to commonly asked questions about our products and services.

What factors affect my auto insurance rates?

+

Your premium is influenced by several factors including your driving record, age, location, vehicle type, and daily mileage. Additionally, your credit history and coverage limits play a role. Maintaining a clean driving record and bundling policies can help lower your rate.

What’s the difference between full coverage and liability only?

+

Liability only covers damage you cause to others, which is the state minimum requirement. "Full coverage" typically refers to adding Collision and Complete coverage to your policy, protecting your own vehicle from accidents, theft, and weather damage regardless of fault.

How much coverage do I need?

+

While state laws set minimums, they are often insufficient for serious accidents. We generally recommend higher liability limits to protect your assets (savings, home) from lawsuits. If you have a financed or valuable car, you will also need Collision and Complete coverage.

Can I save money on my auto insurance?

+

Yes! You can lower your premium by bundling with home or renters insurance, increasing your deductibles, maintaining a good driving record, and taking advantage of discounts for safe driving, good grades (for students), and vehicle safety features.

What should I do after an accident?

+

First, ensure everyone is safe and call emergency services if needed. Exchange information with the other driver and document the scene with photos. Then, contact us immediately to start your claim. Our 24/7 support team will guide you through the process.
